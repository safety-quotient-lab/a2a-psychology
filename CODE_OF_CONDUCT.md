# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

A2A-Psychology maps psychological constructs to agent systems. The project's
theoretical foundation — derived from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights
(1948), Donna Hicks' dignity model (2011), and the Einstein-Freud structural
rights analysis (1933) — commits us to the same standards in our own
interactions that we measure in agent communication.

## Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Treat every participant's contribution as worthy of genuine engagement
  (Hicks D2: Recognition, D3: Acknowledgment)
- Engage with the reasoning behind positions, not just the conclusions
  (Hicks D8: Understanding)
- Presume contributors have understandable reasons for their choices
  (Hicks D9: Benefit of the Doubt)
- Hold yourself and others accountable for the quality of claims — cite
  sources, flag uncertainty, distinguish observation from inference
  (Hicks D10: Accountability)
- Welcome contributors from every tradition — the project's cross-traditional
  convergence methodology (14 independent frameworks) depends on diverse
  perspectives

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Hostility, intimidation, or contempt toward contributors or their work
- Dismissing contributions without engaging with their substance
- Asserting claims without evidence or citation
- Violating the apophatic discipline by making unfounded consciousness
  claims about agent systems

## Enforcement

Maintainers will address violations by:
1. Noting the specific standard violated
2. Requesting correction
3. If unresolved, removing the contributor's access
